Time Travel Theories

The Many-Worlds Interpretation

Written by: Okabe Rintarou

The Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) is an interpretation of quantum mechanics that asserts that the universal wavefunction is the only fundamental entity and that there is no wavefunction collapse. This implies that all possible outcomes of quantum measurements are physically realized in some "world" or universe.

Replies:

John Titor: This theory aligns closely with my experiences in time travel. Each decision spawns a new worldline, with countless worldlines existing in parallel.

Makise Kurisu: While the MWI is fascinating, it's essential to approach it with skepticism. The lack of empirical evidence makes it hard to validate against other quantum interpretations.

SERN: We're watching closely. Theories like these could have implications for our own experiments.

Black Hole Information Paradox

Written by: Kurisu Makise

The Black Hole Information Paradox is one of the most significant unresolved problems in theoretical physics. When particles are swallowed by a black hole, what happens to the information they contain? Some believe it's lost forever, while others think it may be emitted as Hawking radiation.

Replies:

Okabe Rintarou: If information is truly lost in a black hole, it defies the principles of quantum mechanics. The universe is indeed a complex puzzle!

John Titor: Black holes might be the key to successful time travel. This information paradox could be the very thing that makes it possible!

Quantum Entanglement and Spooky Action at a Distance

Written by: Moeka Kiryu

Quantum entanglement is a phenomenon where the states of two or more particles become correlated in such a way that the state of one particle cannot be described independently of the state of the other particles. Einstein referred to it as "spooky action at a distance."

Replies:

Kurisu Makise: Quantum entanglement could be the foundation for quantum computing. It's truly fascinating!

SERN: Our research into quantum entanglement is top secret. This is dangerous knowledge...

Time Dilation and Special Relativity

Written by: Suzuha Amane

Time dilation is a difference in the elapsed time measured by two observers, due to a velocity difference between them, or to a difference in gravitational potential between their locations. It's a core concept in Einstein's theory of relativity.

Replies:

Okabe Rintarou: So, if I travel fast enough, I can effectively travel to the future? El Psy Kongroo!

John Titor: Time dilation is very real. I've experienced it firsthand during my time travels.
